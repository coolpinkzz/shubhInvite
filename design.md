# ShubhInvite - Wedding Invitation

## Overview

Design a **cinematic digital wedding invitation** — not a generic template, but a masterpiece. When someone opens it, they should think: _"I've never seen an invitation like this."_

**Theme:** luxury, elegant, minimal, Indian, premium.

**Brand personality:** Apple restraint × Cartier golden refinement × Indian royal warmth. The experience should feel sacred, serene, and sumptuous — like stepping into a misty temple at dawn.

**Target audience:** Wedding guests receiving a premium digital invite on mobile (primary) and desktop.

**Emotional tone:** Reverent, romantic, refined. Never loud, never cluttered, never kitschy.

**Platform:** Mobile-first responsive web (320px minimum). Full-screen sections. Cinematic scroll — no boring document-style layouts.

**Sample couple (use for all mockups):**

- Groom: **Arjun**
- Bride: **Priya**
- Wedding date: **December 14, 2026**
- Location: **Udaipur, Rajasthan**
- Hindi accent: **शुभ विवाह**

---

## Colors

The palette is rooted in warm ivory foundations, royal maroon depth, Cartier gold accents, and subtle emerald touches.

- **Ivory (#fdfbf7 – #ebe3d5):** Primary page backgrounds. Warm, paper-like, never pure white. Creates the feeling of handmade parchment and temple marble.
- **Royal Maroon (#5c1a1a – #2a0a0a):** Primary brand color. Used for headlines, hero overlays, dark sections, and ceremonial weight. Evokes sindoor, velvet, and royal Rajasthani textiles.
- **Cartier Gold (#d4af37 – #c9a962):** The sole accent for sacred moments — couple names, dates, CTAs, ornamental borders, and dividers. Use sparingly; gold should feel earned, not decorative noise.
- **Emerald (#2d6a4f – #0f2e24):** Secondary accent for subtle highlights, badges, and nature/temple greenery. Use rarely.
- **Charcoal (#1a1a1a – #6b6b6b):** Body text and muted captions. Never use pure black.
- **Rose (#f5e1e4):** Soft blush accent for flower petal overlays and gentle warmth.

**Color usage rules:**

- Light sections: ivory background + maroon/charcoal text + gold accents
- Dark sections: maroon-900 background + ivory text + gold accents
- Gold gradient text only for names, dates, and one ceremonial heading per section
- Maintain WCAG AA contrast (4.5:1 minimum for body text)

---

## Typography

Three-font system with clear hierarchy:

- **Cormorant Garamond (Display):** Couple names, section titles, ceremonial headings. Elegant serif with high contrast strokes. Light to medium weight. Feels editorial and timeless.
- **Inter (Body):** Paragraphs, event details, form labels, metadata. Clean, readable, modern. Regular and medium weights only.
- **Pinyon Script (Accent):** Romantic flourishes — "With Love", "Under the stars", "शुभ विवाह". Use once per section maximum. Never for body text.

## Layout

**Structure:** Single-column, full-viewport sections stacked vertically. Each section is a cinematic "scene" — not a card grid dashboard.

**Container widths:**

- Mobile: full bleed with 20–24px side padding
- Tablet: max 768px centered
- Desktop: max 1024–1280px centered

**Section spacing:** Generous vertical rhythm. Sections breathe — minimum 80px padding top/bottom on mobile, up to 160px on desktop. Never cram content.

**Grid patterns:**

- Story section: 50/50 split on desktop (photo left, text right); stacked on mobile
- Timeline: Vertical card stack with connecting line
- Gallery: Horizontal scroll strip with large images
- RSVP: Centered single column, max 480px

**Safe areas:** Account for mobile notch and bottom navigation. Keep CTAs and music controls in thumb-reachable zones.

---

## Elevation & Depth

Depth is achieved through **layered surfaces and soft shadows**, not heavy drop shadows.

- **Soft shadow:** `0 2px 20px -4px rgba(26,26,26,0.06)` — default cards
- **Elevated shadow:** `0 8px 40px -8px rgba(26,26,26,0.10)` — hover states, floating cards
- **Luxury shadow:** `0 20px 60px -12px rgba(92,26,26,0.15)` — hero cards, Save the Date
- **Gold glow:** `0 4px 24px -4px rgba(201,169,98,0.35)` — gold buttons, ornamental frames

**Atmospheric depth:**

- Soft fog/mist overlays on temple backgrounds (10–20% white opacity gradient)
- Subtle film grain texture on ivory backgrounds
- Parallax on gallery images and hero particles
- Glass/frosted cards: `backdrop-blur` with ivory at 80% opacity for overlays

**Flat hierarchy alternative:** Use gold ornamental borders and maroon section backgrounds to separate scenes without shadows.

---

## Shapes

- **Corner radius:** 8px (md) for buttons and inputs; 16px (xl) for cards; 24px (2xl) for large feature cards
- **Ornamental frames:** Double-border royal frames with gold or maroon outlines and small corner flourishes (✦, lotus motifs, temple arch hints)
- **Dividers:** Ornamental center-flourish dividers (✦) between sections; dotted and gradient variants for sub-sections
- **Buttons:** Rounded rectangles, never pill-shaped unless badge
- **Images:** Rounded corners (12–16px) or full-bleed within frames
- **Calendar (Save the Date):** Rounded card with subtle gold border, large date numeral

## Animations & Motion

All motion should feel **slow, luxurious, and intentional**. Respect `prefers-reduced-motion`.

| Animation          | Use                          |
| ------------------ | ---------------------------- |
| Fade In            | General content reveal       |
| Fade In Up         | Section entrances on scroll  |
| Fade In Down       | Hero title descent           |
| Scale In           | Cards, calendar, envelope    |
| Reveal             | Text line-by-line            |
| Float              | Particles, petals, Om symbol |
| Shimmer            | Gold button hover            |
| Gold Gradient Text | Couple names, dates          |

**Timing:** 300ms (fast interactions), 500ms (reveals), 800ms (cinematic entrances).
**Easing:** `cubic-bezier(0.16, 1, 0.3, 1)` for reveals; `cubic-bezier(0.25, 0.1, 0.25, 1)` for luxury feel.

## Imagery & Iconography

**Photography style:** Warm, golden-hour tones. Temple architecture, marigold garlands, royal Rajasthani palaces, soft bokeh. Never stock-photo cheesy smiles.

**Illustrations:** Minimal line art — lotus, temple bell, Om, peacock feather, mandala corners. Gold or maroon on ivory. Never cartoonish.

**Icons:** Thin stroke, 1.5–2px weight. Lucide or similar. Gold or charcoal color.

**Backgrounds:**

- Hero: Misty temple at dawn (Udaipur-style architecture)
- Dark sections: Deep maroon with subtle texture
- Light sections: Ivory with film grain noise overlay

**Do not use:** Clip art, emoji-heavy design, bright pink/purple Bollywood gradients, cluttered mandala backgrounds.

## Do's and Don'ts

### Do

- Design mobile-first (375px iPhone as primary artboard)
- Use full-viewport cinematic sections with generous whitespace
- Apply gold sparingly — names, dates, one CTA per section, ornamental accents
- Alternate light (ivory) and dark (maroon) sections for rhythm
- Use ornamental dividers (✦) between content blocks
- Keep typography hierarchy clear: one display size per section
- Design the opening ceremony sequence as a cohesive immersive moment
- Use real sample content (Arjun & Priya, December 14, 2026, Udaipur)
- Maintain 44px minimum touch targets on all interactive elements
- Export with clean layer names matching sections (Hero, Story, Countdown, etc.)

### Don't

- Don't create a generic wedding template or Canva-style layout
- Don't use more than 3 colors prominently in one section
- Don't use Pinyon Script for body text or long paragraphs
- Don't use heavy drop shadows or Material Design elevation
- Don't use pill buttons, neon colors, or gradient backgrounds everywhere
- Don't cram timeline, venue, and RSVP on one screen
- Don't use cluttered mandala or paisley patterns as full backgrounds
- Don't use pure white (#FFFFFF) or pure black (#000000)
- Don't design as a multi-column dashboard or admin panel
- Don't add navigation bars, sidebars, or hamburger menus — this is a scroll journey
